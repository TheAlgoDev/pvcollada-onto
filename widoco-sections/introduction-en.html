Photovoltaic (PV) system modelling software — pvlib, PVsyst, SAM, and their data exchange formats — represents system topology and geometry in incompatible ways. The PV-Collada Ontology addresses this gap by providing a shared, FAIR semantic layer derived from the openly specified PV-Collada 2.0 XML schema.

PV-Collada describes a PV power plant across three interlocking layers that this ontology mirrors directly:

  • Layer 1 — Product library. Typed component specifications (modules, inverters, MPPT units, cables, combiners, optimizers) drawn from manufacturer datasheets.
  • Layer 2 — Circuit topology. Instantiated electrical elements and their wiring: module strings, MPPT inputs, inverter instances, DC/AC combiners, and cable connections.
  • Layer 3 — Physical layout. 3-D rack and tracker geometry encoded as COLLADA scene-graph primitives: racks, tables, posts, gaps, and a triangulated terrain mesh.

The pvc:ModuleLayout class acts as a cross-domain bridge, binding a module's position in the circuit (Layer 2) to its physical slot on a rack (Layer 3). This design supports both electrical simulation queries and spatial/shading analysis within a single knowledge graph.

Formal alignment with MDS-Onto enables downstream interoperability with other materials and built-environment ontologies in the MDS-Onto ecosystem and with upper-level ontologies (BFO, PMDco) through the MDS-Onto hierarchy.
